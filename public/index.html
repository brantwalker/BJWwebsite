About Me

I am a pre-doctoral research fellow at the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research. My background is in mathematics and economics, and I am passionate about using economic tools, including modeling and econometrics, to address real-world issues in our complex world. My work centers around energy & resource issues, specifically water & electricity markets. I also analyze the impact of public policy on human health. Currently, I work with Prof. Frank Wolak at the Program on Energy and Sustainable Development.

Outside of work, I love to read & listen to audiobooks on my daily bike ride (find me on Goodreads: brant-walker). I play ice hockey & football (soccer) about once a week, and play some bad chess for fun (Lichess: ecokingsman). I get out on the trails when I can - the small town Iowa boy cannot stray too far from nature. Oh, I started long-distance running: my first half-marathon was the 2021 Golden Gate Half & I plan on running the 2022 Toronto Marathon in May! And finally, you can always shock and awe me with a cool fact about the oceans (sharks are insane!) and space. If I chose a different career path, I would be a) building deep-sea observation subs b) building/trying to sneak my way onto a spaceship, or c) baling hay.
